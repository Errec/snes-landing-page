Introduces The Shoulder Buttons

Six action buttons with Left and Right triggers add new input combinations

"Nintendo’s next trend-setting" - IGN

"X" and "Y"

Not just two buttons on the face of the controller, but four. "A" and "B" for the original buttons, and "X" and "Y", named after axis alignment planes. Like the Game Boy, the buttons were offset diagonally.

Beyond NES

The directional pad wasn’t Nintendo’s sole contribution to controller design. Aping the NES controller’s basic layout, the Super NES controller had four variable action buttons.
